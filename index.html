Phonics colouring books for every child — with sound built into every page, and the calm that helps kids who find busy books hard.

Most phonics books are loud. Bright colours, busy pages, dense type. Some kids breeze through that. Others — and there are many — shut down before they’ve started.

SoundBloom is what a phonics book looks like when calm comes first. Cream pages instead of glaring white. One letter, one picture, lots of space. Every page has a small QR code that plays the sound out loud — so the hardest part of reading, blending the sounds, doesn’t depend on a parent already knowing phonics. It works for any child. It works especially well for the ones who find regular books too much.

the approach

Five channels, one page.

A child learns a sound more durably when it arrives through more than one sense at the same time. Every SoundBloom page is designed to activate at least three.

Visual

The big colourable letter, the object beside it, and the group colour. The child sees the same layout every page, so they don't have to relearn where things live.

Auditory

Each QR code plays the sound on demand, in a warm voice — slower if you toggle it. Nothing autoplays.

Tactile-Motor

Colouring the letter and the picture activates fine motor learning. Thick 4-point outlines, never thin lines.

Phonological

The SAY IT strip cues the sound three times. No spoken response is ever required — pointing counts.

Kinaesthetic

Dot-to-dot tracing pages let the child write the shape of the letter, building muscle memory while they hear the sound.

around the books

The book is the centre. These hold it up.

An app made just for the books.

Every QR opens the SoundBloom Sounds app — a tiny ad-free web app that plays just that sound. No install. No account. No tracking. No 'up next' to pull your child into a tunnel. One sound per screen, that's the whole product.

Future-proof QR codes.

Every QR in every book points to a redirect link we own. If a sound ever needs replacing or moving, the redirect updates and the book still works. The link your child scans today will still work in 2040.

Completion you can feel.

A sticker chart at the back of every book. One sticker per page completed. The chart fills up as your child progresses — completion as a felt, visible thing.

The sounds are also mirrored on a free YouTube channel — for parents who prefer their child meet the content there.

the app

Inside the SoundBloom app.

A companion to the books — never a replacement. Plays sounds from a QR scan, runs short calm modules, awards medals that can't be taken away, and tracks progress on the device. No account, no ads, no data sent anywhere unless you explicitly turn on sync.

Home

Does

  • Plays sounds from a QR scan — warm voice, three repetitions, optional slower speed.
  • Runs short calm modules: Hear → See → Trace → Find → Blend → Celebrate, ~5–8 minutes.
  • Awards medals — Seed, Star, Bloom, Streak, Gold, Diamond, Quiet, Together. Never taken away.
  • Lets up to 4 child profiles share one device — no account needed.
  • Adapts pacing gently — notices which sounds your child engages with most.
  • Weekly summary + downloadable progress PDF, useful for IEP documentation.
  • Works offline once a sound has been opened. Free, forever — every feature, every book.

Doesn't

  • Send data without explicit opt-in. Cloud sync is opt-in only.
  • Require an account, login, or email to start.
  • Show ads, recommendations, or "up next" suggestions.
  • Have premium tiers, in-app purchases, or paywalls.
  • Autoplay between sounds — your child finishes when they finish.
  • Show leaderboards or compare children to anyone else.
  • Use red marks or "wrong" indicators — no failure states for the child.
  • Make sounds without consent — every audio event requires a tap.

As much as the child needs. Never more than that.

questions parents ask

Six things, answered honestly.

What age is this for?+

Broadly, three to seven. The pace and visual restraint also work for older children with reading delays or sensory needs — we've designed it so an eight-year-old won't feel patronised picking it up.

Isn't this just more screen time?+

Each sound takes about 30 seconds in the app. The parent holds the phone. The book is the main event — the screen is a thin assist, not a babysitter. Total screen time per book: about 13 minutes spread over weeks.

Does my child need to know the alphabet already?+

No. Book 1 starts from zero. Each letter is introduced as a sound first, a shape second, and a picture third — in that order, deliberately.

Is this evidence-based?+

The design follows widely-accepted principles of accessible print — readable typography, low visual density, generous whitespace, restrained colour. It's not a clinical intervention and we're not making medical claims. It's just careful design.

Will the QR codes still work in five years?+

Yes. Every QR points to a redirect link we own, not directly to the SoundBloom Sounds app or YouTube. If anything ever needs updating, the redirect updates and the book still works.

Who designed this?+

A small independent project — no organisation behind it yet, no clinical credentials being claimed. We made the book we'd want for the kids in our own lives. If it helps yours, that's the whole point.
